Pierogi Ruskie

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Abigail Koet
  • Filling Type: Potato and Cheese
  • Recipe Yield: 48 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 10 minutes
  • Diet: Diet Restricted

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp lard
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup water

Filling Ingredients

  • 4 oz farmer cheese or cottage cheese
  • 5 potatoes
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion chopped or grated
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Dough Preparation

  1. Add the flour and salt to a large bowl; whisk to combine.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat water and the lard over medium-high until lard is melted.
  3. Pour the liquid into the flour gradually, stirring it in as you add it. (The dough will be crumbly at this point.)
  4. Stir in the egg until combined then move the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth.
  5. Cover the dough with a dampened towel or plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

Filling Preparation

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut into 1-inch cubes. Add them to a large pot, cover with potatoes with water.
  2. Bring to a boil over high and continue to cook until potatoes are tender, and can be broken easily with a fork.
  3. While the potatoes cook, prepare the onions. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium-high. Add the onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, until sofented and golden-brown.
  4. Push cheese through sieve into a large bowl.
  5. Drain potatoes and mash. Add cooked onions to potatoes and stir thoroughly. Add potato mixture to cheese, and add generous amount of salt and pepper. Stir to combine. Set aside and let cool.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Cut Dough into 2 even pieces (leave one piece under the towel or wrapped to keep moist.)
  2. Dust flour onto work surface and onto a baking sheet (for holding the pierogi)
  3. Roll out dough until about 1/8 an inch thick
  4. Using an inverted glass or a 3-inch cookie cutter, punch 12 disks of dough.
  5. Working with one disk at time, spoon a tablespoon and a half of filling into the middle of it.
  6. Fold the dough in half to enclose the filling. Pinch the two sides together at the top, then work your way down on both sides, pinching the dough over the filling and pushing in the filling as needed.
  7. If needed, you can dip your fingertip into water and moisten the dough in spots as needed to help the two sides adhere together.
  8. Repeat with other half of dough.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. To cook the pierogi, add a single layer of pierogi to a pot of boiling water. Let them cook until they rise to the surface, about 3 minutes.
  2. Use a slotted spoon to transfer cooked dumplings
  3. Boil remaining dumplings.
  4. Working in batches, melt 1 to 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet. Add a few boiled pierogi in a single layer to avoid overcrowding, and cook until crisp and golden, 2 to 3 minutes per side.
  5. Repeat with remaining pierogi, adding butter as needed.

Mushroom and Cabbage Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Edward Cynar
  • Filling Type: Mushroom and Cabbage
  • Recipe Yield: 36 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 25 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 small head of cabbage
  • 1/3 cup watter
  • 1 large onion
  • 4 ounces can of mushroom stems and pieces
  • 2 tbsp butter or margarine
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Instructions

Dough Preparation

Filling Preparation

  1. Combine cabbage, water, onion, mushrooms and butter in a saucepan.
  2. Cook covered, over low heat until tender, about 30 minutes.
  3. Add Salt and pepper and mix well.
  4. Set aside and let cool.
  5. Note: Sauerkraut can be substituted for cabbage.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Dust flour onto work surface and Roll out dough until about 1/8 an inch thick
  2. Using a 3-inch cookie cutter, cut 12 circles of dough.
  3. Spoon about 2 tablespoon of filling into the middle of dough cutouts.
  4. Fold the dough in half around the filling. to enclose the filling.
  5. Seal edge with water.
  6. Press edges with fork.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. To cook the pierogi, add a single layer of pierogi to a pot of boiling water.
  2. Let them cook until they rise to the surface, about 3 minutes. Continue to boil until puffy, about 3 more minutes.
  3. Use a slotted spoon to transfer cooked dumplings
  4. Boil remaining dumplings.
  5. Can be eaten boiled, but can be fried in butter or lard to be made golden and crisp. About 4 minutes both sides.

Meat Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Frances Stachelski
  • Filling Type: Ground Meat
  • Recipe Yield: 36 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 13 minutes
  • Diet: Diet Restricted

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 3 cups flour
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cup water

Filling Ingredients

  • 2 onions
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cups ground cooked meat
  • 2 slices stale white bread
  • milk or water
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Instructions

Dough Preparation

Filling Preparation

  1. Chop onions finely.
  2. Stir-fry chopped onions in butter in a skillet for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add cooked ground meat, cook a couple more minutes.
  4. Remove from heat.
  5. Soak stale bread in just enough milk or water to cover.
  6. When thoroughly soaked (about 10 minutes), squeeze out excess liquid.
  7. Stir bread, salt, and pepper into meat mixture until well combined.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Dust flour onto work surface and Roll out dough until about 1/8 an inch thick
  2. Using a 3-inch cookie cutter or glass, cut 12 circles of dough.
  3. Spoon about a tablespoon of filling into the middle of dough cutouts.
  4. Close dough in half around the filling.
  5. Seal edge with water, and press edges tightly together.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. To cook the pierogi, add a single layer of pierogi to a pot of boiling water.
  2. Let them cook until they rise to the surface, about 3 minutes. Continue to boil until puffy, about 3 more minutes.
  3. Use a slotted spoon to transfer cooked dumplings
  4. Boil remaining dumplings.
  5. Can be eaten boiled, but can be fried in butter or lard until golden. About 2 minutes for each side.

Dough Ingredients

Potato Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Irene Ostrowski
  • Filling Type: Potato
  • Recipe Yield: 48 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 45 minutes
  • Cook Time: 10 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp salt

Filling Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 2 cups mashed potatoes

Instructions

Dough Preparation

Filling Preparation

  1. Sautee onion in pan with butter over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until tender and golden.
  2. Stir in salt and pepper.
  3. Combine mashed potatoes and onion mixture.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Dust flour onto work surface
  2. Divide dough into 4 portions
  3. Roll out one portion of dough until about 1/8 an inch thick.
  4. Using a round cookie cutter, cut-out disks of dough.
  5. Working with one disk at time, spoon about 2 tsps of filling into the middle of each disk.
  6. Fold in half.
  7. Seal edges with fork
  8. Repeat with other portions of dough.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Boil a pot of water.
  2. Don't over-crowd pierogies, boil until they float.
  3. Remove pierogies from water.
  4. Boil remaining pierogies.
  5. Fry pierogies in butter until golden brown.
