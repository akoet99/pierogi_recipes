Blueberry Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Frances Chrzanowski
  • Filling Type: Blueberry
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian
  • Substitutions: Blueberries can be substituted for other berries or apple.

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups Flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Filling Ingredients

  • 2 cups blueberries
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp fine bread crumbs

Instructions

Dough Preparation

Filling Preparation

  1. Combine blueberries, water, and sugar in a saucepan.
  2. Bring to a boil; cook and stir until fruit is tender and water is almost gone.
  3. Remove from heat.
  4. Mash fruit slightly with potato masher.
  5. Add cinnamon and lemon juice.
  6. Cook and stir over low heat just until fruit mixture is thick.
  7. Stir in enough breadcrumbs to make filling very thick.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Add a tbsp of filling into middle of dough pieces.
  2. Fold dough in a half moon shape, and press edges tightly together.
  3. If necessary, you can use a bit of water on the tip of finger to get edges to stick together better.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  2. Drop pierogi in water, you don't want to have any touching.
  3. Boil until the pierogi float, and boil additional 5 minutes, or until dough becomes puffed.

Sweet Quark Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Eleanor Kosinski
  • Filling Type: Cheese
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 45 minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp oil

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups pot cheese, farmer cheese, or ricotta
  • 1 egg
  • tbsp sugar3
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

Instructions

Dough Preparation

Filling Preparation

  1. Press cheese through a sieve into a large bowl.
  2. Add remaining ingredients and mix well.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Add a heaping tbsp of filling to each cutout.
  2. Fold dough in half around filling.
  3. Press seam with spoon or or fork.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Boil pierogi for about for minutes, they should float.
  2. Pierogi can be enjoyed boiled, or you can fry the boiled pierogi in butter until golden on both sides.
