Blueberry Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Frances Chrzanowski
  • Filling Type: Blueberry
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian
  • Substitutions: Blueberries for other berries or apple.

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups Flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Filling Ingredients

  • 2 cups blueberries
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp fine bread crumbs

Instructions

Dough Preparation

  1. Bring a kettle or pot of water to a boil. Pour 1/3 cup of water and set aside to cool long enough that you can touch it.
  2. While water is cooling, combine flour and salt in a large bowl.
  3. Make a well in the center of flour and drop in egg and oil. With a fork or your hand, mix egg into flour.
  4. Pour the hot water over flour mixture and immediately mix in with hand.
  5. Knead dough into a ball. Continue to knead dough on counter until stretchy.
  6. Cover dough with upside down bowl. Let dough rest covered on counter at room temperature for about 20 minutes before rolling out and filling.

Filling Preparation

  1. Combine blueberries, water, and sugar in a saucepan.
  2. Bring to a boil; cook and stir until fruit is tender and water is almost gone.
  3. Remove from heat.
  4. Mash fruit slightly with potato masher.
  5. Add cinnamon and lemon juice.
  6. Cook and stir over low heat just until fruit mixture is thick.
  7. Stir in enough breadcrumbs to make filling very thick.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Dust flour onto work surface and Roll out dough until about 1/8 an inch thick
  2. Using a cookie cutter or glass, cut 12 circles of dough.
  3. Add a tbsp of filling into middle of dough pieces.
  4. Fold dough in a half moon shape, and press edges tightly together.
  5. If necessary, you can use a bit of water on the tip of finger to get edges to stick together better.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  2. Drop pierogi in water, you don't want to have any touching.
  3. Boil until the pierogi float, and boil additional 5 minutes, or until dough becomes puffed.

Prune Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Rita Stachelski
  • Filling Type: Prune
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 4-6 minutes
  • Diet: Vegan

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 cup prunes
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions

Dough Preparation

  1. Add the flour and salt to a large bowl, whisk to combine.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat water.
  3. Add eggs to flour, whisk slightly.
  4. Pour warm water into the flour gradually, stirring it in as you add it.
  5. Knead until dough until smooth.
  6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.

Filling Preparation

  1. Soak prunes overnight.
  2. Cook soaked prunes with sugar and lemon juice in skillet over medium heat until they break apart with a wooden spoon.
  3. Remove from heat.
  4. When cool, remove stones.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Roll dough out very thin, on a floured surface.
  2. Cut out round pieces from dough.
  3. Add a tsp scant of filling into the middle of each circle.
  4. Wet edges of dough, then fold in half. Press edges firmly, you can use a fork if you like.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Boil a pot of water.
  2. Boil pierogi, they should float and then begin to puff.
  3. When pierogi are puffy, remove from water.

Apple Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Pauline Ziranko
  • Filling Type: Apple
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 1 hour
  • Cook Time: 20 minutes
  • Diet: Vegan

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Filling Ingredients

  • 6 apples
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

Instructions

Dough Preparation

  1. Dump flour into a bowl and make a well in the middle.
  2. Add salt
  3. Add water into well and mix into flour and salt.
  4. Continue until dough forms.
  5. May need to add more water, do this 1 tbsp at a time.
  6. Knead dough until elastic.
  7. Let dough rest under a towel for at least half an hour. Can be refrigerated up to 2 days.
  8. If refrigerating dough, bring dough to room temperature before rolling out.

Filling Preparation

  1. Peel and grate apples, put grated apples in bowl.
  2. Mix in sugar and cinnamon.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Flour surface.
  2. Divide dough in half.
  3. Working with the first half, roll dough out very thin, to the point you can almost see through it.
  4. Cut out circles, and gather remaining dough to roll out again.
  5. Repeat with remaining dough.
  6. Add a tbsp of filling to each circle.
  7. Fold dough in a half-moon shape. Then crimp edges shut.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  2. Working in small batches, parboil pierogies until they float. Approx. 3 minutes.
  3. Boil pierogies additional 4 minutes.
  4. Remove from water and repeat with remaining pierogies.

Sweet Quark Pierogi

  • Cusisine: Polish-Traditional
  • Creator: Eleanor Kosinski
  • Filling Type: Cheese
  • Recipe Yield: 24 Pierogies
  • Prep Time: 45 minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Diet: Vegetarian

Ingredients

Dough Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp oil

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups pot cheese, farmer cheese, or ricotta
  • 1 egg
  • tbsp sugar3
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

Instructions

Dough Preparation

  1. Combine flour and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Make a well in the center of flour and add oil. Slighty mix with a fork.
  3. Pour water over flour mixture and mix in with hand.
  4. Knead dough into a ball. Continue to knead dough on counter until elastic.
  5. Cover dough with damp towel. Let dough rest covered on counter at room temperature for 30 minutes.

Filling Preparation

  1. Press cheese through a sieve into a large bowl.
  2. Add remaining ingredients and mix well.

Assembling Pierogi

  1. Divide the dough into 2 parts.
  2. Onto a lightly floured surface, roll out the first piece of the dough thinly, to a thickness of approx. 1/16 inch.
  3. Use a cup or a pierogi/pastry cutter to cut out rounds.
  4. Add a heaping tbsp of filling to each cutout.
  5. Fold dough in half around filling.
  6. Press seam with spoon or or fork.

Cooking Pierogi

  1. Boil pierogi for about for minutes, they should float.
  2. Pierogi can be enjoyed boiled, or you can fry the boiled pierogi in butter until golden on both sides.
